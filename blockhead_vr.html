Goal

In the course Material Design at Simon Fraser University, the final project was to create a virtual reality headset using two material, in our case wood and metal. The team consisted of Riley Schina, Robert Hodan, Ernie Tsang and I. We had four weeks to complete the project and the process included ideation, sketching, prototyping, designing and building.

Problems

  • Weight of headset
  • Comfort
  • Secure
  • Audio source

Decision

  • Counterbalance the weight
  • Use metal
  • Headphones for audio

Bhupinder's sketches during the ideation process.

Solution

Pinewood

  • Cheap
  • Light
  • Durable

Aluminum

  • Flexible
  • Light
  • Cheap

Helmet straps

  • Quick release
  • Adjustable
  • Cheap

Audio

  • Bluetooth earbuds
  • Doesn't interfere with headset

The final prototype made by the team.

Render of Blockhead VR to further enhance the model; created by Ernie.

Reflection

This project taught me the value of sketching and prototyping. The sketches helped with planning ahead and staying on track to complete the project, and prototypes assisted in building the headset. My idea of the helmet straps worked very well since it did not add extra weight and was strong enough to hold. Furthermore, I was part of the ideation, prototyping and building process. I was able to add key features to the infographic such as materials and human factors.
